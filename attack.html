Malware

Malware Attack

Malware is malicious software designed to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to systems. Types include viruses, worms, trojans, and spyware.

Phishing

Phishing

Cybercriminals trick users into giving away sensitive information using fake emails, websites, or messages that look legitimate.

Ransomware

Ransomware

Ransomware encrypts your files and demands payment for their release. Paying the ransom does not guarantee data recovery.

Denial-of-Service

DoS/DDoS Attack

Attackers overload systems or networks, making them inaccessible for legitimate users. DDoS attacks use multiple sources for greater impact.

MITM

Man-in-the-Middle (MITM)

The attacker secretly relays and possibly alters the communication between two parties who believe they are directly communicating.

SQL Injection

SQL Injection

Malicious SQL code is inserted into a database query, allowing attackers to access or modify data they shouldn't be able to.

Brute Force

Brute Force Attack

Automated software is used to guess passwords or encryption keys by trying all possible combinations until the correct one is found.

Password Attack

Password Attack

Techniques such as credential stuffing, dictionary attacks, and keylogging are used to gain access to user accounts.

Zero-Day

Zero-Day Exploit

Attackers exploit unknown vulnerabilities before developers can issue a fix, making these attacks highly dangerous.

Social Engineering

Social Engineering

Manipulating people into giving up confidential information by exploiting human psychology rather than technical hacking.

Drive-by Download

Drive-by Download

Unintentional download of malicious software when visiting a compromised website or clicking on a deceptive link.

DNS Spoofing

DNS Spoofing

Attackers redirect web traffic by corrupting DNS records, leading users to fake sites without their knowledge.

Session Hijacking

Session Hijacking

The attacker steals a user's session token to gain unauthorized access to their account or sensitive data.

XSS

Cross-Site Scripting (XSS)

Attackers inject malicious scripts into trusted websites, affecting users who visit those sites.

Eavesdropping

Eavesdropping Attack

Unauthorized real-time interception of private communication, such as emails or voice calls.

Supply Chain

Supply Chain Attack

Attackers compromise software or hardware providers, infecting their products before they reach end-users.
